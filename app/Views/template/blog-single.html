Top 10 Universities for Computer Science in Europe: A 2024 Guide

Sarah Jenkins Senior Education Consultant
Oct 24, 2023
schedule 8 min read

European universities are increasingly becoming the top choice for computer science students worldwide. The combination of rigorous academic standards, affordable tuition, and rich cultural immersion makes these programs uniquely attractive compared to their US counterparts.

In recent years, the landscape of technology education has shifted. While Silicon Valley remains a powerhouse, Europe's tech hubs in Berlin, London, Zurich, and Amsterdam are fostering a new generation of innovators. This guide breaks down the institutions leading this charge.

Why Choose Europe for CS?

Beyond the obvious allure of travel, studying in Europe offers tangible career benefits. Many programs are now taught entirely in English, removing language barriers for international students. Furthermore, the Bologna Process ensures that degrees are recognized globally.

Ranking Criteria

Our ranking methodology focuses on three core pillars: Research Output, Industry Connections, and Student Satisfaction. We analyzed data from over 200 institutions to bring you this definitive list.

  • Research Output: Number of citations per faculty member.
  • Industry Connections: Partnership programs with companies like Google, Spotify, and SAP.
  • Student Satisfaction: Based on the annual EuroStudent survey results.

1. ETH Zurich (Switzerland)

Consistently ranked among the top 10 globally, ETH Zurich is the crown jewel of European engineering. Its computer science department is renowned for work in AI and robotics.

The main building of ETH Zurich.

2. University of Oxford (UK)

While steeped in history, Oxford's CS department is thoroughly modern. The university focuses heavily on the theoretical underpinnings of computer science, preparing students for deep research roles.
